### 1. Solution to the Asked Question (For Exam)

**Q. 2. a) Explain the levels of planning (strategic, tactical, and operational) with suitable examples highlighting their differences and interconnections. (7 Marks)**

**Answer:**

**Introduction:**
Planning is the primary function of management that involves setting goals and determining the best course of action to achieve them. In any organization, planning is not a single activity but is performed at multiple levels. The three primary levels of planning are Strategic, Tactical, and Operational.

**1. Strategic Planning (Top-Level Management):**
*   **Meaning:** Strategic planning is the process of determining the long-term vision, mission, and overarching goals of the entire organization. It is done by top-level management (CEOs, Board of Directors).
*   **Timeframe:** Long-term (usually 3 to 5+ years).
*   **Example:** A major tech company (like Apple) making a strategic plan to transition all their computers from Intel processors to their own custom Silicon chips (M-series) over the next 5 years.

**2. Tactical Planning (Middle-Level Management):**
*   **Meaning:** Tactical planning breaks down the broad strategic plan into specific, actionable goals for individual departments or divisions. It is conducted by middle-level managers (Department Heads, Regional Managers).
*   **Timeframe:** Medium-term (usually 1 to 3 years).
*   **Example:** Based on the strategic goal above, the Hardware Engineering Department creates a tactical plan to design the architecture of the new chip and secure supply chain partnerships within the next 18 months. Meanwhile, the HR department creates a tactical plan to hire 500 new chip designers.

**3. Operational Planning (Lower-Level/First-Line Management):**
*   **Meaning:** Operational planning involves the day-to-day or week-to-week execution of tactical plans. It focuses on specific tasks, schedules, and routines and is handled by first-line managers (Team Leads, Supervisors).
*   **Timeframe:** Short-term (daily, weekly, or monthly).
*   **Example:** An engineering team lead creates an operational plan in the form of a 2-week "Agile Sprint." The developers are assigned daily tasks to write specific lines of code or test specific components of the new chipâ€™s software drivers.

**Differences among the Levels of Planning:**

| Feature | Strategic Planning | Tactical Planning | Operational Planning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Management Level** | Top Management (CEO, Board) | Middle Management (Dept. Heads) | Lower Management (Team Leads) |
| **Time Horizon** | Long-term (3-5+ years) | Medium-term (1-3 years) | Short-term (Days to 1 year) |
| **Scope** | Entire organization | Specific departments/divisions | Specific units or individuals |
| **Focus** | Vision, mission, and survival | Resource allocation and department goals | Day-to-day routine execution |
| **Uncertainty** | High risk and high uncertainty | Moderate risk | Low risk, highly predictable |

**Interconnections between the Levels:**
The three levels are highly interdependent and operate as a unified system:
*   **Top-Down Guidance (Cascading):** Strategic plans set the boundaries and direction. Tactical plans cannot exist without a strategic goal, and operational plans are merely the detailed steps required to complete the tactical plans. (Strategy dictates Tactics, Tactics dictate Operations).
*   **Bottom-Up Execution and Feedback:** The actual work is done at the operational level. If an operational plan fails (e.g., a software bug delays the sprint), it impacts the tactical timeline, which in turn could delay the overall strategic goal. Feedback flows upward so top management can adjust the strategy if necessary.

### 2. Core Concept Explanation (Romanized Nepali + English)

*Bholi exam ma sajilo hos bhanera yo concept lai simple tarika le bujhau.*

**Levels of Planning bhaneko k ho?**
Kunai pani thulo company ma ekai thau ma sabai plan banidaina. Mathi CEO dekhi tala ko team leader samma sabai le a-aafno level ma plan garchan. Yeslai 3 bhag ma baadiyeko cha:

**1. Strategic Planning (Thulo Sapana / Top Level):**
*   *Concept:* Yo bhane ko company ko main goal ho. CEO ra Board of Directors le banauchan. Yo lamo samay ko lagi huncha (3-5 barsa).
*   *Nepali/English:* Sochnus, euta Nepali IT company (jastai eSewa) le plan garyo: "Aba ko 5 barsa ma hami Nepal ko sabai bhanda thulo AI-based digital wallet banchau." Yo thulo vision lai **Strategic Plan** bhancha. Yesma risk dherai huncha ra total company ko future yesmai depend garcha.

**2. Tactical Planning (Department ko Target / Middle Level):**
*   *Concept:* Mathi ko thulo sapana pura garna har-ek department (HR, IT, Marketing) le aafno aafno plan banauchan. Yo 1 dekhi 3 barsa ko huncha.
*   *Nepali/English:* Aba eSewa ko "AI wallet" banaune thulo plan (Strategic) pass bhayo. Tespachi IT Department ko head le plan garcha: "Aba ko 1 barsa ma maile AI architecture design garna parcha." Uta HR department le plan garcha: "Aba ko 6 mahina ma 20 ota naya Machine Learning Engineers hire garna parcha." Yo department-wise plan lai **Tactical Plan** bhancha.

**3. Operational Planning (Daily Kaam / Lower Level):**
*   *Concept:* Yo bhane ko ground level ma hune daily wa weekly plan ho. Team lead wa supervisor le banauchan.
*   *Nepali/English:* Aba tyo hire gareko 20 jana engineer haru lai IT Manager le daily task dincha. "Timile yo week bhitra login page ko API sakne, timile database check garne." Yo 1-2 week ko 'Sprint' wa daily routine lai **Operational Plan** bhancha.

**Differences (Pharak k cha?):**
*   **Time:** Strategic long-term, Tactical medium-term, Operational short-term.
*   **Manche:** Strategic boss le, Tactical department head le, Operational team lead le.

**Interconnections (Ek-aapas ma kasari jodieka chan?):**
*   Tinta level eutai sikri (chain) ka kadi haru hun.
*   **Top-down:** Mathi CEO (Strategic) le goal nadee kana IT department (Tactical) le k kaam garne thaha paudaina.
*   **Bottom-up:** Tala developer (Operational) le code lekhera daily task pura garena bhane, IT department ko 1 barsa ko target (Tactical) fail huncha. Tyo fail bhayo bhane CEO ko 5 barsa ko thulo sapana (Strategic) pani fail huncha. Sabai kura ek aapas ma connected cha!
